## Note on Artificial Intelligence:
While the mathematics and engineering behind AI is fascinating, (I want to major in this stuff!) I do not think it mixes well with art.

I consider computer science as an art where you can express yourself via means of cognition. I've come across bizarre and extraterrestrial code that coincidentally came from extraordinary people. Point is, there isn't one way to solve a problem! Artificial Intelligence, from its optimized-nature, tends to regurgitate and repeat logical thinking. (dubbed as "mode collapse") How I see it, the beauty in mathematics and coding is how well it allows you to express yourself! Reducing all creative thinking to just one quick and "right" solution undermines the value in solving a problem authentically.

I became disillusioned with CS once I saw the severity of AI's abstractions. Don't get me wrong, we've been abstracting for all of mankind! Math beget physics. Physics beget circuitry. Circuitry beget computers. etc. It is not necessary to master the knowledge behind all these layers just to type print("Hello World") in a python file. (which it, too, has its own abstractions!) However, I think people in the CS commmunity undermine the value of letting their imagination run wild. Bureaucracy and a result-driven world has detached value from this artistic rhetoric of STEM. I _want_ to see spaghetti code. I _want_ to see one-liner programs. I _want_ to see chaotic code as long as it **tells** me something about the author! 

Maybe it is a privileged perspective to have the resources and time to pursue STEM artistically. In some scenarios, it is completely valid and necessary to capitalize on Artificial Intelligence. Not everything needs to (or should) be artistic. There's some days where you really don't want to learn the taxonomy of heuristics. (metaheuristics???) Artificial Intelligence, as with other technology, just makes life easier! I cannot stress how great of a time-saver it is when you have greater matters to deal with. Touché. 

There wouldn't be such a controversy in the role of AI if it wasn't nuanced. This brief rant is just my two-cents on the status-quo and I felt it to be important enough to plaster it over my Github front page.

If nothing else, this serves as a preface to my profile. Some of my code (especially in solo-projects) is devoid of comments, hard-to-follow, and likely inefficient. I'm always open to suggestions and explaining parts of my works.
